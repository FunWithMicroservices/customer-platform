Frequently Ask Questions

How long is the notice period?

You may terminate the contract at the end of the insurance year. The termination is only effective if it is received at least one month before expiry.

What do the no-claims classes mean?

The more years you get through everyday life without any claims, the cheaper your car insurance becomes and the more money you save. If you have an accident, you move into a lower and therefore more expensive no-claims class - and your premium increases. So the more carefully you drive, the more it pays off for you. More importantly, the less you and other road users are harmed.

What is the difference between fully comprehensive and partially comprehensive insurance

Sounds complicated, but it is quite simple: Liability insurance pays for all damage caused to other vehicles and people, plus financial losses. In addition, we clarify the question of guilt for you and defend against unjustified claims. Partial comprehensive insurance, on the other hand, pays for damage to your own vehicle such as: Theft, natural forces such as storm, hail or lightning, collision with animals of all kinds, glass breakage and repair, animal bites, damage caused by short circuits and much more. Details can be found in our overview of benefits. The fully comprehensive insurance additionally covers all damages to your own car caused by self-inflicted accidents or vandalism. Fully comprehensive insurance is the best protection for valuable and new vehicles as well as young used cars.

When do the no-claims classes change?

We reclassify each contract at the main due date (January 1 or start of the season) of each year according to its claims history in the previous calendar year. If the contract was free of claims during a calendar year and the insurance coverage was uninterrupted during this time, the contract will be classified in the next better SF class. If the contract has been subject to claims during a calendar year, it will be downgraded according to the respective downgrading table.
